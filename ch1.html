Three misconceptions about Computer Science:

  1. Computer science is the study of computers
  2. Computer science is the study of how to write computer programs
  3. Computer science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software

Briefly define:

  1. Computer Science- the study of algorithms, icluding their mathmatical properties, hardware and linguistic realizations, and their applications
  2. Algorithm- a collection of unambiguous and effectively computable operations that produces a result
  3. Sequential operation- a single well-defined task that when finished moves to the next operation
  4. Conditonal operation- a question and a follow up operation based on the answer
  5. Iterative operation- used to repeat opertaions
  6. Stored program computer- intructions are represented as binary string and stored in memory
  7. Programmer- a person who writes commands in a high-level language
  8. Intergrated circuit- silicon with transistors, resistors, and capacitors, that replace the need for discrete electornic compontents

Briefly describe these computer generations

  1. First generation computer- bulky, expensive, slow, and unreliable. vacuum tubes for processing and storage and were difficult to maintain and only used by trained personnel
  2. Second generation computer- reduced size and cost, increased reliability, vacuum tube replaced by transistors and memory on tiny magnetic cores
  3. Third generation computer- reduced to desk-sized, intergrated circuits with transistors, resistors, and capacitors. birth of the software industry
  4. Fourth generation computer- desktop-size, a complete computer system circuit board could fit in your hand, computer networks, computers in many more things
  5. Fifth generation computer- mini computers everywhere, larger storage, high-resolution grapchics, faster computing

FORTRAN:

  1. Formula Translation
  2. 1954
  3. General purpose programming, mainly intended for mathematical computaitions
  4. Fortran is still used in scientific computing

COBOL:

  1. Common Business Oriented Language
  2. 1959
