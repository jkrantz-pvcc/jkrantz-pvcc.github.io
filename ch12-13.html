Chapter 13: Simulation & Modeling

Define these terms:

  1. scientic method- observing the behavior of a system and formulating a hypothesis that tries to understand and explain that behavior
  2. computational model- a model constructed using algorithmic procedures implemented as computer programs
  3. discrete event simulation- a computational modeling technique that simulates that behavior of a system only at discrete points in time
  4. List and describe seven reasons to construct a simulation model
    • existence- the system might not exist
    • physical realization- the system is not constructed from entities that can be represented by physical objects
    • safety- could be too dangerous to experiment on the actual system or a physical replica
    • speed of construction- might take too much time to construct a physical model
    • time scale- some physical systems change too slowly or too quickly
    • ethical behavior- some physical models have serious moral and ethical consequences
    • ease of modification- easier to test and change than a physical model
